Projects must also be able to show an innovative step in both additive manufacturing and connected digital manufacturing.

If you are a small or micro business, you could receive up to 70% of your eligible project costs. If you are a medium-sized business you could get up to 60%, or 50% if you are a large business.

Projects must range in size from £500,000 to £1.5 million.

If you are a business, you can invite research organisations to work with you on your project.

There will be a briefing event for potential applicants on 7 June 2016.

Background

Additive manufacturing is a process that converts 3D design data into manufacturing instructions. It is game-changing technology that offers huge potential for UK businesses but there are barriers to growth. Recent research and other technology advances can help to resolve some of these barriers.

Globally the market for additive manufacturing products was worth $4.1 billion in 2014 and this figure is growing annually by 35%. We estimate that the UK can win around 5% of this market. It’s a market that is set to reach £70 billion by 2025.

Innovate UK has already invested over £30 million in additive manufacturing projects. This new competition is looking to address some of the remaining technical challenges.
